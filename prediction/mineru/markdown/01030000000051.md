# Current Situation: 2001-2019

Filipino women are still very much a minority in the formal political sphere. It can also be observed that in executive positions such as the cabinet, few women are appointed, especially during President Fidel Ramos’s time, compared to Cory Aquino’s administration (Table 1). As mentioned above, the Philippines has made significant strides in legislating for women’s rights. However, 35 years after redemocratization and 84 years after the grant of suffrage, participation of women in politics is still a work in progress, as in most countries.
